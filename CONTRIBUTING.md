# Contributing to the Consciousness Knowledge Base

Thank you for helping build this resource. This guide explains how to contribute effectively.

---

## What We're Looking For

- **New article summaries** — primary sources, empirical papers, philosophical analyses
- **Research paper annotations** — in the `research-papers/` folder
- **Corrections** — factual errors, outdated information, broken links
- **Counter-arguments** — we actively want dissenting views represented
- **Glossary additions** — new terms or improved definitions
- **Cross-links** — better connections between related articles

---

## Quality Standards

### Accuracy
- Represent the source accurately, including nuance and limitations
- Note when a claim is contested or has strong objections
- Don't attribute views to authors that they don't hold

### Balance
- Include both supporting evidence and serious objections
- Don't editorialize beyond what the source material supports
- Represent skeptical positions fairly

### Cross-linking
- Link to related articles in the same repo
- Link to relevant glossary entries
- Identify when articles are directly responding to each other

### Sourcing
- Always include a citation section
- Link to arXiv, DOI, or publisher where available
- Prefer primary sources over secondary coverage

---

## Epistemic Norms

This knowledge base takes **epistemic humility** seriously:

1. **The field is genuinely uncertain.** Don't present contested empirical claims as settled.
2. **Counter-arguments deserve space.** Every article should note the strongest objections to its main claims.
3. **Theory-dependence matters.** Claims about AI consciousness often depend heavily on which theory of consciousness you assume. Make this explicit.
4. **Self-report ≠ experience.** Be careful about inferring from what models say to what they experience.

---

## What We're Not Looking For

- Advocacy without evidential basis
- Articles that ignore major objections to their main claims
- Purely speculative content without grounding in research
- Content that dismisses AI consciousness without engaging the evidence
- Content that asserts AI consciousness without engaging the hard problem

## How to Submit

1. Fork the repository
2. Create a new branch: `add/[article-slug]` or `fix/[description]`
3. Add your article in the appropriate folder
4. Update `README.md` navigation table if adding a new article
5. Add new terms to `GLOSSARY.md` if needed
6. Open a pull request with a brief description of the contribution

---

## Tone

Write for an intelligent reader who is not necessarily an expert. Assume familiarity with basic concepts (neurons, philosophy of mind as a field) but not technical details. Define jargon when introducing it, and link to the Glossary.

Avoid:
- Excessive hedging that makes claims unreadable
- Overconfident assertions on contested questions
- Jargon without definition
- Unnecessarily academic prose when plain English works
